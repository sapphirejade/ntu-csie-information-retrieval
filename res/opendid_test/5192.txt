CLINICAL:
Lesion left temple suture 12 o'clock. 
MACROSCOPIC:
Specimen labelled "Lesion left temple stitch 12 o'clock", consists of an orientated ellipse of skin measuring 25 x 21mm to a depth of 7mm with a suture attached to one pole marking 12 o'clock. The 3 o'clock margin is inked green and the 9 o'clock margin is inked black.  There is an ulcerated hyperkeratotic lesion in the centre of the skin measuring 15 x 15mm which appears clear of all margins. Block 1- 2TS from 3-9 o'clock; 2 - LS 6 o'clock margin; 3- LS 12 o'clock margin.  (TO: OS/ec  14.12.65)
MICROSCOPIC (Reported by Dr O Plotzker):
Sections show skin and subcutis.  There is a well differentiated squamous cell carcinoma which arises from the epidermis and invades to the deep dermis.  Maximum depth of invasion is 3mm.  No lymphovascular or perineural invasion is seen.  Excision is complete.  The closest side excision margin is 3.5mm (9 o'clock).  The deep margin is free of involvement.
DIAGNOSIS:
Left temple:  Well differentiated squamous cell carcinoma.
